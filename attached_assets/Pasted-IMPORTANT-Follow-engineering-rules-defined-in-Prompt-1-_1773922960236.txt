---

CRITICAL BUSINESS PRINCIPLE

Cashier shift is a BUSINESS-DAY entity, not a calendar-day entity.

Shifts may legally cross midnight (e.g., 11 PM → 7 AM), but must still belong to a single logical business day.

---

BUSINESS DATE RULE (STRICT)

Each shift MUST have:

business_date = logical operating day (not system date at every moment)

Definition:

- business_date is assigned ON SHIFT OPEN
- it NEVER changes during the shift lifecycle
- it does NOT auto-update at midnight

---

MIDNIGHT CROSSING RULE

System must correctly handle shifts that cross midnight.

Valid examples:
- Shift opens at 23:00 → closes at 07:00 next day → SAME business_date
- Shift opens at 16:00 → closes at 01:00 next day → SAME business_date

STRICT RULES:

- A shift must NOT become stale just because system date changed
- A shift becomes stale ONLY if:
  - business_date < current business day
  - AND shift is still open beyond allowed boundary

---

STALE DETECTION (CORRECTED)

System must NOT use naive:

system_date > shift_date → stale

Instead must use:

- configurable business boundary OR
- logical comparison based on business_date

REQUIRED BEHAVIOR:

- If shift started yesterday but is still within allowed operating window → NOT stale
- If shift remained open beyond acceptable duration (e.g., next business cycle) → mark as stale

---

FORBIDDEN BEHAVIOR

- Marking a valid overnight shift as stale at 00:00
- Forcing shift closure just because of date change
- Allowing a shift to continue indefinitely across multiple days

---

REQUIRED DESIGN IN PLAN

Plan must define:

1) Business Day Model
- what defines a business day
- how system determines "today" vs "shift business day"

2) Midnight Handling Logic
- how shift behaves across 00:00
- when shift is still valid vs stale

3) Stale Criteria (STRICT)
- clear rule when shift becomes stale
- must include time-based + business_date logic

4) Maximum Shift Duration Rule
- define safe max duration (e.g., 12–18 hours or configurable)
- prevent 2-day open shift scenario

5) Safe Close Behavior Across Midnight
- closing after midnight must still be valid
- must not require forced transfer due to date only

SHIFT OWNERSHIP RULE (STRICT)

Every shift operation must validate:

assertShiftOwnership(shiftId, userId)

If violated:
→ 403 Forbidden

No exception.

---

MULTI-SHIFT RULE

System must support multiple active shifts simultaneously.

- Shift A → Cashier 1
- Shift B → Cashier 2

Both may operate concurrently without conflict.

---

INVOICE OWNERSHIP RULE (STRICT)

Invoices must be assigned deterministically:

- first valid claim wins
- ownership is atomic
- no duplicate ownership
- no UI-based race decisions

Use:

claimed_by_shift_id
claimed_at

---

CLOSING RULES (STRICT)

1) target_shift must NOT equal current_shift
2) target_shift must be OPEN and VALID
3) if pending invoices exist:
   - closing allowed ONLY if another valid shift exists
4) if only one shift exists:
   - closing MUST be blocked
5) no silent transfer
6) every transfer must be logged

---

ATOMIC CLOSING (MANDATORY)

Close operation must be atomic:

- UPDATE status = "closing"
- revalidate
- commit OR rollback

No TOCTOU allowed.

---

TRANSFER LOG (MANDATORY)

Every transfer must be recorded:

cashier_transfer_log:
- from_shift_id
- to_shift_id
- invoice_ids
- transferred_at
- transferred_by
- reason

No implicit transfers.

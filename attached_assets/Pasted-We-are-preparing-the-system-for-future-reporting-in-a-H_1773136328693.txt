We are preparing the system for future reporting in a Hospital ERP system.

IMPORTANT:
Do NOT modify existing business logic or transactional tables.
We only want to design a reporting architecture for future reports.

The reporting layer must support the FULL PATIENT JOURNEY across the hospital.

PATIENT JOURNEY FLOW
Patient → Registration → Admission / Visit
→ Medical Services (Lab, Radiology, Procedures, ICU, OR)
→ Pharmacy
→ Billing / Invoice
→ Payment
→ Discharge

STEP 1 — Analyze existing transactional tables, especially:

Patient / Clinical

- patient_invoice_headers
- patient_invoice_lines
- services

Financial
- journal_entries
- journal_lines
- sales_invoice_headers
- sales_invoice_lines

Inventory / Pharmacy
- inventory_lots
- inventory_lot_movements

Other operational tables if present.

STEP 2 — Identify tables expected to grow heavily in production:
For example:
- journal_lines
- inventory_lot_movements
- patient_invoice_lines
- sales_invoice_lines

Explain which tables will become reporting bottlenecks.

STEP 3 — Design a dedicated REPORTING LAYER that avoids heavy joins on transactional tables.

Create proposed reporting tables such as:

Patient Journey
- reporting_patient_visit_summary

Financial

Operational

Pharmacy

STEP 6 — Define a REFRESH STRATEGY for each reporting table:

Options:
